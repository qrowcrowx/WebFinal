"FOR THE GLORY AND THE FALL"



League of Legends is a complex strategic gameplay that requires skill, teamwork and decision making to compete who would dominate. With over 150 unique champions, each ability and playstyle is different and therefore might take some time to learn every character in game but at least the game provides wealthy options for players to explore which playstyle or role might be suitable for them. The game still receives consistent update and balancing although it has few bugs here and there. Balance adjustments and quality of life improvements are often being implemented to keep the players playing.



Just in case you get bored for playing the normal Summoners Rift 5v5 map, you can play the other gamemode they offer which is ARAM (All Random All Mid), a 5v5 one lane game unlike the original Summoners Rift which has 3 lane. The Champion you pick is random, giving you opportunity to play champions you haven't tried and in game, there is no recall to heal and buy items. As soon as you leave the base, you can no longer regen from your Base and buy Items until you are defeated. ARAM games are typically shorter than the Summoners Rift so if you would like to play a quick match, ARAM is your best bet.

A League of Legends matches typically range from 20 to 50 minutes and therefore it usually take a lot of your time, it requires a time commitment to finish a single game. To investe in a substantial amount of time for a single match can be exhausting for the player. Furthermore the game's addicitve nature to climb up the rank ladder may lead to much more extended play sessions potentially affecting other aspecs of your life. And like every multiplayer game, League of Legends heavily relies on teamwork to win along with proper communication to execute proper in-game experience and win.



For gamers looking for a rich and intense multiplayer experience, League of Legends comes highly recommended. Its continued appeal is a result of its strategic gameplay, vibrant esports scene, and regular upgrades. However, some players who are seeking for a more laid-back or solo-focused experience can be turned off by the challenging learning curve, time commitment, and need on teammates.
