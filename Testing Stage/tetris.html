Tetris NES (1989) is a game Developed and published by Nintendo and it was based on Alexey Pajitnov's Tetris (1985). Tetris has always been popular until this day, several more remakes are being made including the recent relase Tetris 99. Tournaments are still active since Tetris can be played with two-player, it is a competition of which scoring the highest without losing (in which case the player has no Space left to play)


The players complete lines by moving variously shaped pieces (tetrominoes) that fall onto the playing field. The finished lines vanish and award the player points, and the player can then fill in the empty areas. When the uncleared lines reach the top of the playing field, the game is over. The longer the player can postpone this consequence, the greater their score. In multiplayer games, players must outlast their opponents.


The NES Version tetris has two modes which you can play, A-Type and B-Type. In A-Type, the goal is to achieve the highest score. The level advances as lines are cleared, increasing the speed of the falling blocks. While in B-Type, The board starts with randomized obstacle blocks at the bottom of the field at the start of a B-Type game, and the goal is to clear 25 lines. The level remains fixed in B-Type, and the player chooses the height of the obstacle ahead of time.


Now, Should you play Tetris for fun? for competitive gameplay? We recommend anyone that is interested to try tetris to give it a shot even for casual matches and solo gameplay Tetris will make sure you'll have fun throughout your whole playthrough. Tetris actually improves our thinking skills and level of analysis, For the earlier versions of tetris the player cannot hold a piece so they will have to put the piece in a way it will not disrupt your space this allows the player to think where to place every piece to gain more points by doing more tetris combos and of course through all that process, your level of analysis also improve.
