"EMBRACE THE ABYSS"



Barotrauma is a single/co-op survival game published by Daedalic Entertainment. Being a survival game, it is similar to games like FTL: Faster Than Light and Space Station 13 which are both Sci-Fi games. What makes Barotrauma unique is that it is a horror submarine simulator that combines both sci-fi and horror aspects with the presence of terrifying alien sea monsters while keeping it familiar with 2D ragdoll physics.


Barotrauma is a game that requires teamwork, communication, and cooperation in order to get through the dark depths of Europa. Although it is encouraged to play with multiple friends Barotrauma can be played as in single player mode, which allows you to control bots in the form hireable units with the use of marks, the game's primary form of currency. Going back to the co-op side of the game, Barotrauma will test your ability to adapt in multiple situations, whethere your submarine is under attack by numerous hammerhead, or invaded by pesky mudraptors, it is up to you, the player, alongside your friends- crewmates rather, to survive the dangers lurking in the seas of Europa.


Europa is an ocean with a frozen layer on top, requiring the need for submarines to traverse between outposts and various waypoints. But what started humanity's venture towards one of Jupiter's moon? Well, in the future, the amount of habitable places started to run out, Europa being a frozen ocean meant that humanity could thrive in it, with the condition that they live under the ice-cold surface, avoiding the harsh environment above. However, humanity isn't alone in this planet for there are creatures lurking underneath, willing to combat with the occupants of the submarines travelling across the dark depths.



There are multiple roles that players can take when joining a campaign. The first is the captain which is the main commander of the submarine, tasked to navigate the sub in the depths of Europa while calling out any abnormalities seen in the sonar, as well as commanding other personnel. Next up are the mechanics and the electrical engineers, the foundation of the submarine's funtionality. These two roles can be distinguished by their jobs, with the mechanic being task to repair the hull and mechanical devices, while the electrical engineers maintain the main power supply of the sub called the reactor while keeping the junction boxes in proper condition. Then, the medic and the security officer comes in, which have tasks in rescuing and giving aid to other crewmates when under peril. Security officers are armed with weapons built to combat the hostile creatures of Europa, while Medics have expertise to in making sure that each member survives from their injuries. Lastly, the assistant which are all-around members that can take on most tasks, making them a jack-of-all trades or a pain by being worshippers of the honkmother as clowns.

Overall, Barotrauma is a great experience to build teamwork and coordination while having fun with the goofy ragdoll physics as your submarine gets taken apart but the various monsters lurking in the deep. It is a game that could still be played by single player enjoyers, but it definitely more enjoyable when played alongside friends, willing to travel across the biomes of Europa and discovering the mystery of the dark depths of the frozen ocean.
