"Fun-Strating Mountain Climbing"



The controls in Bread & Fred follow a simple-to-learn, hard-to-master principle. You and your partner are connected by a rope and must move together throughout the game. You have the ability to move left or right, jump, pick up items, interact with characters, anchor yourself, and swing your partner. The first penguin you encounter provides initial instructions, and you'll encounter this penguin in various other locations as you progress to new areas.


You quickly learn the basic controls for movement, picking up and throwing your partner to reach higher places. Soon, you come across platforms that require swinging your partner in circles around you to generate enough momentum to propel both of you to the next ledge. Later on, you learn how to grab walls and allow your partner to swing to another wall or platform, enabling you to progress further.

With these controls in mind, your objective is to ascend the mountain as high as possible. The journey is not easy, as there are nearly 500 meters to climb. Along the way, you encounter new areas, additional obstacles like ice, mud, and moving platforms, and challenging jump placements. Some platforms are so small that they can only accommodate one character.


The game features pixelated designs, but the environment remains clear and offers a suitable and somewhat scenic atmosphere. The backgrounds are drawn rather than pixelated, giving the game an art design that doesn't feel completely old-school. The music complements the game with a fitting style that fills the background without becoming distracting or overpowering the sound effects. This was a good choice by the developers.


There is some replayability in Bread & Fred. You can always improve your skills, and there are hidden areas to discover throughout the climb. Given the extensive climbing required in the game and the limited number of hidden areas, it's easy to miss them. Additionally, the experience of playing in single-player mode differs significantly from multiplayer mode, with both modes offering their own enjoyable aspects.


It's unfortunate that Bread & Fred only offers local cooperative play, as it feels like a missed opportunity. Many players, including myself, were hoping for online multiplayer functionality. The option to use Remote Play on Steam to play with a friend is not an ideal solution, as it comes with its own frustrations and is not designed specifically for multiplayer gaming. Adding online capabilities to the game should be strongly considered by the developers.



The checkpoint system, while useful, should not be something that players have to unlock. It could have been integrated into the control explanation provided by the starting character. Nevertheless, it's a positive feature that can be turned off if desired, although players who prefer not to use it can simply refrain from pressing the checkpoint and recall buttons, as these buttons have no other function.

Bread & Fred is a challenging yet entertaining game that is definitely worth playing. While patience is required to overcome the journey's difficulties, the sense of accomplishment when conquering challenging sections is truly satisfying. The inclusion of a checkpoint system removes much of the frustration typically associated with these types of games and makes it enjoyable to challenge oneself in this compact game. I highly recommend Bread & Fred for both its single-player and multiplayer experiences.
